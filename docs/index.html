Uncertaintext Example

Uncertaintext is designed to be simple to use, but with enough flexibility to show your uncertain numbers just the way you want them. For example, here is an uncertain number with a normal distribution with a mean of 10 and standard deviation of 2, updated twice a second, and displayed as an integer:

The HTML element that accomplishes this miracle is a little verbose, but pretty easy to understand:

<span class=uncertaintext data-uct-distrib=normal data-uct-mu=10 data-uct-sigma=2 data-uct-format=".0f" data-uct-fps=2></span>

Next, let's take some of uncertaintext's flexibility out for a spin. The table below shows that same uncertain number with a different formats (in the rows) and refresh rates (in the columns).
